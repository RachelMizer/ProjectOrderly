# Orderly Team Roles & Responsibilities

## Important Note About Roles

**These roles represent areas of focus and expertise, NOT exclusive ownership.**

All team members will contribute across different areas of the project. These role assignments simply identify:

- **Who leads** in specific domains
- **Who to consult** when questions arise in that area
- **Who coordinates** related tasks and maintains quality standards in their focus area

Everyone on the team will code, test, document, and collaborate. The roles below indicate where each person will **dedicate extra attention** and serve as the **go-to resource** for the team.

------

## 1. Product Owner

**Team Member:** Kim Mayo

### Definition:

The Product Owner represents the voice of the customer and stakeholders. They focus on maximizing the value of the product and guiding prioritization decisions to ensure the team builds the right features in the right order.

### Areas of Focus:

- **Guide product vision and feature prioritization** - Help the team understand what's most valuable and why
- **Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria** - Ensure everyone understands what "done" means for features
- **Represent user needs** - Keep customer perspective front and center during decisions
- **Validate completed work** - Review features to confirm they meet business requirements
- **Facilitate trade-off discussions** - Help the team balance scope, time, and quality
- **Maintain stakeholder communication** - Serve as primary contact with Professor Tabbal and mentor
- **Keep backlog organized** - Ensure user stories remain clear and prioritized

**What this means:** Kim leads discussions about *what* we build and *why*, but everyone contributes to understanding user needs and validating features.

## 2. Software Architect

**Team Member:** Tristin Gatt

### Definition:

The Software Architect designs the high-level structure of the system and makes critical technical decisions about how components interact, ensuring the system is scalable, maintainable, and secure.

### Areas of Focus:

- **Design overall system architecture** - Define how frontend, backend, database, and cloud components work together
- **Guide technology selection** - Lead decisions on frameworks, libraries, and tools (React, Django, AWS, etc.)
- **Create and maintain architectural diagrams** - Document system structure, data flow, and component relationships
- **Establish technical standards** - Define coding conventions, folder structure, API design patterns
- **Review technical approaches** - Provide guidance when team members face architectural questions
- **Identify technical risks early** - Anticipate scalability, security, or integration challenges
- **Ensure consistency across codebase** - Help team maintain coherent technical vision

**What this means:** Tristin leads technical design decisions and provides architectural guidance, but everyone implements and contributes ideas to the architecture.

### Areas of Focus:

- **Guide test planning** - Help team understand how to test features effectively

**What this means:** Kenny leads quality assurance efforts and coordinates testing activities, but everyone writes tests and participates in quality validation.

## How These Roles Collaborate in Practice

### Example: Implementing "Product Browsing Interface"

| Role                                   | Contribution                                                 |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Assigned Developer (Rachel)**        | Implements the feature, writes initial tests                 |
| **Product Owner (Kim)**                | Clarifies what customers need to see, defines success criteria |
| **Software Architect (Tristin)**       | Guides API design, data fetching approach, component structure |
| **Frontend Development Lead (Rachel)** | Coordinates React component design, ensures UI consistency   |
| **QA Testing Lead (Kenny)**            | Ensures comprehensive test coverage                          |
| **Technical Writer (Caleb)**           | Documents the feature, reviews code comments, updates user guide |
| **Presentation Lead (Tyler)**          | Captures screenshots and progress for sprint review demo     |
| **DevOps Lead (Team)**                 | Ensures feature deploys correctly, monitors performance      |
| **Scrum Master (Serina)**              | Tracks progress, removes blockers, facilitates discussions   |

**Key Point:** Kim owns the implementation, but everyone contributes their expertise to make it successful.

------

## Key Takeaway

**Roles = Leadership & Coordination, Not Ownership**

- Everyone is a **developer** first
- Roles identify **areas of expertise and responsibility**
- Team success requires **collaboration across all domains**
- When in doubt, **ask the person with that role focus** for guidance

------

This approach prevents silos while maintaining clear accountability and expertise in critical areas.
