1. Getting Started

The Support Dashboard

When you log in with a Support account, you land on the Support Dashboard — your central starting point for the day. The dashboard is divided into three live sections that refresh each time you load the page, along with a status bar and team presence list in the sidebar.

At the top of the main content area, a date bar shows today's date as a quick reference. Below it, three dashboard sections are stacked in order of priority: Pick Up Where You Left Off, New Tickets, and Assigned to Me. The sidebar runs alongside all pages and provides navigation, your current status, and the team presence list.

Pick Up Where You Left Off

The first section on the Support Dashboard tracks the tickets you have viewed most recently in your current browser. Each row shows the ticket number, subject, status badge, priority badge, and the date the ticket was opened. Click any row to jump directly back to that ticket.

The list is stored locally in your browser and holds up to ten entries. It updates automatically each time you open a ticket, always placing the most recently viewed one at the top. If you clear your browser's local storage — or if a listed ticket has since been deleted — stale entries are quietly removed the next time the dashboard loads.

If you have not yet opened any tickets in your current browser, this section shows a message inviting you to open a ticket to get started.

New Tickets and Assigned to Me

The New Tickets section lists every ticket currently in New status — tickets that have been submitted but not yet opened or assigned. These are the tickets most in need of immediate attention. Click any row to open the ticket and begin working it.

The Assigned to Me section lists every open ticket currently assigned to your account. This is your personal workload at a glance. Like the other sections, each row is clickable and takes you directly to the ticket detail page.

Both sections pull live data from the server each time the dashboard loads. If either section has nothing to show, a message is displayed in its place.

Setting Your Status

Your availability status is displayed in the sidebar and is visible to all other members of your support team. Three statuses are available to set from the sidebar: Online, Busy, and Away. A fourth status, Offline, is applied automatically by the system when you log out and cannot be set manually while you are logged in.

To change your status, locate the status bar in the sidebar above the Support Options section. Click the dropdown and select the status that reflects your current availability. The change takes effect immediately and updates your presence indicator for the rest of the team in real time.

Use Online to indicate you are at your desk and available. Use Busy if you are heads-down on something and should not be interrupted. Use Away if you have stepped away temporarily. Your status is automatically set to Offline when you log out.

Team Presence

At the bottom of the support sidebar, a live list shows all active support team members and their current statuses. Each member appears with a colored presence dot next to their name: green for Online, amber for Busy, and gray for Away or Offline. Online members are displayed in bold, Busy members in italics, and Away or Offline members in a muted style to help you quickly read the team at a glance.

This list refreshes automatically every thirty seconds, so you always have an up-to-date picture of who is available without navigating away from your current page. The same presence information is surfaced in the Team Member Status section of the Support Dashboard.
