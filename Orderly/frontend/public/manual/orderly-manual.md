# Orderly User Manual

---

# 1. Introduction

## What is Orderly

Orderly is a web-based business management platform designed to help small and medium-sized businesses run their day-to-day operations from a single, easy-to-use interface. It brings together order management, product cataloging, inventory tracking, supplier management, and sales reporting under one roof — so you spend less time switching between tools and more time running your business.

At its core, Orderly has two sides: a customer-facing storefront where your customers browse and place orders, and a business dashboard where you manage everything behind the scenes. This manual covers the business dashboard — the tools available to you as the account holder.

Orderly runs entirely in your web browser. There is nothing to install, and your data is accessible from any device with an internet connection.

## Who This Manual is For

This manual is written for business account holders — the people responsible for managing an Orderly-powered store. That includes store owners, managers, and any staff members who have been granted access to the business dashboard.

You do not need a technical background to use Orderly. If you can navigate a website, you can use this platform. This manual walks you through every feature step by step, with plain language and no assumed knowledge.

If you are a customer looking to place or track an order, this manual is not for you — your experience is handled through the storefront, which operates separately from the dashboard described here.

# 2. Getting Started

## Receiving Your Credentials

Once your payment has been processed, Orderly will send your login credentials to the email address you provided during checkout. Check your inbox — including your spam or junk folder — for an email containing your username and temporary password.

With those credentials in hand, you are ready to log in to your business dashboard and get started.

## Logging In

To access your business dashboard, navigate to your Orderly login page and enter the username and password from your credentials email. Click **Log In** to continue.

Once logged in, you will be taken directly to your admin dashboard. From there you can access every tool covered in this manual — orders, catalog, inventory, reports, and settings.

If you cannot log in, double-check that you are entering your credentials exactly as they appear in the email, including any capitalization. If you have forgotten your password, use the **Forgot Password** link on the login page to reset it.

## The Admin Dashboard

After logging in, you will land on the Dashboard Home — your central starting point for managing your store. The dashboard is organized into three areas:

### Pick Up Where You Left Off

This section tracks the pages you have visited most recently within the dashboard — such as a specific order, a product you were editing, or an inventory item you were reviewing. Each entry appears as a card showing the name and the last date you accessed it. Click any card to jump straight back to that page.

### Inventory Alerts

This section always displays your six lowest-quantity inventory items, sorted from least stock to most. Each tile shows the item name, current stock level, and the reorder threshold you have set. Items that are completely out of stock are highlighted in red; items at or below their reorder level are marked in amber. Click the section header to go directly to the full inventory view.

## Navigation Layout

The Orderly admin interface is organized into two persistent areas: a left sidebar and a top bar.

### The Sidebar

The left sidebar displays the **Orderly** brand logo at the top, which you can click at any time to return to the Dashboard Home. Below it, the sidebar is context-aware — its content changes depending on which section of the dashboard you are currently in. When you are working inside a section, the sidebar shows a title, a brief description of what that section does, any relevant quick-action buttons, and a link back to the Dashboard Home.

In the Orders section, the sidebar also displays a list of your recently viewed orders so you can jump back to them quickly.

### The Top Bar

The top bar runs across the top of every page. On the left it shows a welcome message with your account name. On the right you will find three links: **Help**, which opens this user manual; **Account Settings**, which takes you to your personal account page; and **Logout**, which ends your session and returns you to the login screen.

Below the top bar on the Dashboard Home, five large tiles provide quick access to the main sections of your dashboard: **Reports**, **Inventory**, **Product Catalog**, **Orders**, and **Business & Store Settings**.

# 3. Settings

## Business Information

Business Settings is accessible from the Settings Management hub, which you can reach via the **Business & Store Settings** card on the main dashboard. It stores the core operational details for your business that Orderly uses internally and may surface in reports and documents.

The top of the form contains two fields: **Business Name**, which identifies your business within Orderly, and **Tax Rate (%)**, which is applied to orders at checkout. Enter the tax rate as a percentage — for example, enter *7.25* for a 7.25% rate.

The **Headquarters / Home Office Information** section below holds contact details for the business: a multi-line **Address** field, a **Phone Number**, and an **Email** address. These fields are optional but recommended if you intend to use printed or exported documents that should include your contact information.

When you make any changes, an **Unsaved changes** notice appears near the save button as a reminder. Click **Save Settings** to apply your changes. A confirmation message will appear briefly on success.

## Storefront Appearance

Storefront Settings controls everything that affects how the customer-facing side of your Orderly store looks and presents itself. It is divided into three areas: Branding, Customize Your Storefront, and Customize Your Icons.

### Branding

The **Business Logo** field accepts an image file that is displayed as your store's header logo. For best results, use a wide banner-style image with an aspect ratio of approximately 4:1 — a minimum size of 1200×300 pixels is recommended for sharp display on high-resolution screens. PNG format with a transparent background is preferred. Keep the file size under 500KB.

The **Browser Tab Icon (Favicon)** is the small icon that appears in the browser tab next to your page title and in browser bookmarks. It should be a square image, ideally 32×32 pixels (64×64 or 128×128 also work). PNG format is recommended. Keep the design simple — a logo mark, initial, or symbol reads better than a full logo at very small sizes. File size should be under 100KB.

The **Tagline** field holds a short line of text displayed on your storefront header. Use the **Display tagline on storefront** checkbox to show or hide it without deleting the text.

The **Location & Hours** fields — Store Address, Store Phone, and Hours — are displayed on your storefront for customers to reference. The Address and Hours fields are multi-line text areas, so you can format them across multiple lines as needed.

### Customize Your Storefront

The **Font Choice** selector lets you choose the typeface used across your storefront. Six options are available: Arimo, Lora, Roboto, Playfair, Raleway, and Munson. Each option is displayed in its own typeface so you can preview the style before selecting it.

The **Page Style Color Choices** section contains fifteen individual color controls covering all major areas of the storefront — including the page background, header, navigation bar, main content, footer, buttons, and two section accent backgrounds. Each color can be set using the color picker or by typing a hex code directly into the adjacent text field.

A **Live Style Preview** panel updates in real time as you adjust colors and font, showing how the navigation bar, main content area, section accents, buttons, and footer will look together. This lets you assess the overall color scheme before saving.

### Customize Your Icons

The **Customize Your Icons** section lists all of your product categories and lets you assign an emoji icon to each one. These icons appear alongside category names on the storefront's filter controls. Click **Pick Icon** next to a category to open the emoji picker, then browse by category tab or scroll through the full set to find the icon you want. Selecting an emoji saves it immediately — category icons do not require clicking Save Settings. To remove an assigned icon, click **Remove Icon** in the category's row.

Once all settings are configured, click **Save Settings** at the bottom of the page to apply your branding and color changes. An **Unsaved changes** notice appears whenever you have made edits that have not yet been saved.

## Your Account

The Account Settings page is accessible from the **Account Settings** link in the top bar of the admin dashboard. It displays a read-only summary of your account profile, including your full name, username, email address, and a masked password field.

Your account role is shown as a badge at the top of the page. As a business user, your role is **Business User**, which grants access to the full admin dashboard and all of its management features. The **Role & Permissions** card lists the specific areas your account is authorized to access.

Account details such as your name, email, or password cannot be changed from this page. If you need to update your credentials or request additional permissions, contact your Orderly administrator.

# 4. Suppliers

## Viewing the Supplier Directory

The Supplier Management section lists all of your saved suppliers in a single table. Each row shows the supplier's name, email address, and phone number, along with action buttons for editing and deleting. You can reach Supplier Management from the sidebar when you are in the Inventory or Product Catalog sections.

If no suppliers have been added yet, the page shows an empty state message inviting you to add your first supplier. Once suppliers exist, they become available for assignment to individual inventory items.

## Adding a Supplier

To reach Supplier Management, navigate to the Product Catalog and click the **Supplier Management** link in the sidebar. Once there, click the **+ Add Supplier** button at the top of the page to open the add supplier form. The form has three fields: **Supplier Name** (required), **Email** (optional), and **Phone** (optional). Email and phone are contact details only — they are displayed in the directory and on any inventory item the supplier is assigned to, but are not used by Orderly for any automated communication.

Click **Add Supplier** to save the new entry and return to the directory, or **Cancel** to discard the form without saving.

## Editing a Supplier

To edit a supplier, click the **Edit** button in that supplier's row. The row converts to editable fields in place, allowing you to update the name, email, and phone number without leaving the page. Click **Save** to apply your changes or **Cancel** to discard them and restore the original values.

To remove a supplier entirely, click **Delete** in the supplier's row. You will be asked to confirm before the deletion is carried out. Deleting a supplier removes it from the directory permanently; any inventory items that had this supplier assigned will have their supplier field cleared.

## Assigning Suppliers to Inventory Items

Suppliers are assigned to inventory items from each item's detail page. Open the detail page by clicking the item's name in the inventory list, then locate the **Supplier** card. The card contains a dropdown listing all suppliers currently in your directory. Select the appropriate supplier and click **Save** in the Supplier card to apply the assignment.

Once a supplier is assigned, the card displays the supplier's email address and phone number beneath the dropdown for quick reference, so you can contact them without leaving the inventory page.

To remove a supplier assignment, open the same dropdown and choose **— None —** at the top of the list, then save. If the supplier you need does not exist yet, the Supplier card includes a **+ New Supplier** shortcut that takes you directly to the add supplier form. After saving the new supplier there, return to the inventory item's detail page to complete the assignment.

# 5. Categories

## Creating a Category

Categories are used to group your products on the storefront, making it easier for customers to browse your offerings. Before you can add a product to a category, the category must already exist.

To create a new category, navigate to the Categories section and click **Add Category**. Enter a **Category Name** — this must be unique across your catalog. You can also assign an optional **Icon** to the category, which will appear alongside the category name on the storefront. Click **Save** to create the category.

## Editing a Category

To edit an existing category, click **Edit** in its row. You can update the category name and icon. Click **Save** to apply your changes.

Renaming a category does not affect the products assigned to it — they remain associated with the category regardless of the name change.

## Assigning Products to Categories

Products are assigned to categories through the product form. Every product must belong to exactly one category — it is a required field when creating or editing a product.

To change the category a product belongs to, open the product in the catalog using the **Edit** button, select a different category from the **Category** dropdown, and click **Update Product** to save.

## Deleting a Category

To delete a category, click **Delete** in its row and confirm the action when prompted.

A category cannot be deleted while it still has products assigned to it. If you need to remove a category that is in use, first reassign or delete all products in that category, then return to delete it.

# 6. Product Catalog

## Browsing the Catalog

The Product Catalog displays all of your products in a sortable table. Each row shows the product's **ID**, **Name**, **Category**, **Supplier**, and **Description**. Click any column header to sort the list by that column; click it again to reverse the order.

The search bar at the top of the page filters the list as you type, matching against product name, category, supplier, and description. Click **Clear Filters** to remove the search and return to the full catalog.

Each product row has three action buttons. **Edit** opens the product form so you can update its details. **Delete** permanently removes the product. **Edit Options** expands the row in place to show the product's variants — each with its name, SKU, unit price, stock quantity, and reorder level — and gives you a form to add or modify them without leaving the catalog view.

The toolbar also provides quick access to **Export** and **Print** for the full catalog, as well as buttons to create a new product or add a new supplier.

## Adding a New Product

To add a new product, click **Create New Product** in the catalog toolbar. This opens the product creation form.

**Product Name** and **Category** are required. The category must already exist — if you have not yet created the relevant category, set it up in the Categories section first and then return here. **Supplier** and **Description** are optional but recommended for keeping your catalog organized.

The **Options** checkboxes let you indicate whether the product has variants and whether it supports modifier groups. **Has Variants** is ticked by default, as most products will have at least one variant (for example, a size or a default option). **Has Modifiers** should be ticked if customers will be able to make add-on selections when ordering this product, such as extras or customizations. These settings determine what will be available to configure after the product is created.

When creating a new product, the form also includes fields to set up the product's **first variant** straight away: a **Variant Name** (such as Small, 12oz, or Default — the product name is used if left blank), a **SKU**, a **Unit Price**, and a **Stock Quantity**. Filling these in saves you a separate step after saving. Additional variants can be added later from the catalog view.

You can optionally upload a **Product Image** using the file picker at the bottom of the form. A preview is shown once a file is selected. Click **Create Product** to save, or **Cancel** to return to the catalog without saving.

## Editing a Product

To edit an existing product, click the **Edit** button in its row on the catalog page. This opens the same form used to create a product, pre-filled with the product's current details.

You can update the product's name, category, supplier, description, image, and its Has Variants and Has Modifiers settings. When editing, the first-variant fields shown during creation are not present — variants are managed separately using the **Edit Options** panel directly in the catalog view.

Click **Update Product** to save your changes, or **Cancel** to discard them and return to the catalog.

## Variants and SKUs

Variants represent the individual purchasable options of a product — for example, sizes, volumes, or styles. Each variant has its own SKU, unit price, stock quantity, and reorder level, and is tracked independently in your inventory.

You can manage variants in two places. From the catalog view, click **Edit Options** in a product's row to expand an inline panel showing that product's variants, with a form to add a new one. For a dedicated management view across all products, click **🔧 Variants & Modifiers** in the Product Catalog sidebar or in the catalog toolbar — this opens the Variants & Modifiers page, where you can add a new variant by selecting a product from the dropdown and filling in the variant name, SKU, unit price, and optional stock quantity.

To edit an existing variant, click **Edit** in its row. To remove a variant entirely, click **Delete**. Deleted variants cannot be recovered.

Every product should have at least one variant. If a product has only one option with no meaningful name — for example, a standard item sold at a single price — use the product's name or a label like *Default* as the variant name.

## Modifier Groups and Options

Modifier groups allow customers to customise their order when selecting a product — for example, choosing a milk type for a coffee, or selecting add-ons for a meal. Each modifier group belongs to a specific variant and contains a set of options the customer can choose from.

To create and manage modifier groups, click **🔧 Variants & Modifiers** in the Product Catalog sidebar or in the catalog toolbar. On the Variants & Modifiers page, scroll to the **Modifier Groups & Options** section. To add a new group, select the product and then the specific variant it belongs to, enter a group name (such as *Milk Type* or *Extras*), set whether it is **required** or optional, and define the minimum and maximum number of selections a customer must make. Click **Add Group** to save it.

Once a group exists, click **▼ Options** in its row to expand the options panel. From there you can add individual options — each has a name and an optional price adjustment that is added to the variant's base price when the customer selects it. Use the **Edit** and **Delete** buttons to manage existing options.

Modifier groups only appear on the storefront for products that have **Has Modifiers** enabled. If a product does not require customisation, leave this setting unticked and no modifier prompts will be shown to customers.

## Deleting a Product

To delete a product, click the **Delete** button in its row on the catalog page. A confirmation prompt will appear asking you to confirm the action. Click **OK** to proceed or **Cancel** to go back.

Deleting a product permanently removes it and all of its associated variants from your catalog. This action cannot be undone. Orders that already reference the product are not affected — their records remain intact.

# 7. Inventory

## Viewing Inventory Levels

The Inventory section displays all of your stock items in a single table. Each row shows the item name, which products it affects (if any), the current stock level and unit of measure, an availability toggle, editable fields for stock quantity and reorder level, and a Save button.

You can sort the list by item name, current stock, or reorder level by clicking the relevant column header. The search bar at the top filters the list by item name as you type.

Clicking an item's name opens its detail page, where you can view and edit all of its properties in one place, including its unit of measure and assigned supplier.

When any item in your inventory is low or out of stock, a **Product Dependencies** panel appears at the top of the page. This panel lists any products that depend on affected items, with the problem ingredients highlighted in amber (low stock) or red (out of stock), so you can see at a glance which products may be impacted.

## Stock Quantities and Reorder Levels

You can update stock quantities and reorder levels directly from the inventory list without opening the detail page. Each row has an **Update Stock** field and a **Reorder Level** field — edit the values you want to change and click **Save** in that row.

The **Available** toggle gives you a quick way to mark an item as in or out of stock. Toggling an item off immediately sets its stock quantity to zero, making it unavailable. Toggling it back on prompts you to enter a new stock quantity before saving.

For more detailed editing, click the item's name to open its detail page. From there you can update the stock quantity, unit of measure, and reorder level together in a single save. The unit of measure options are Units, Ounces, Pounds, Grams, Milliliters, and Litres.

The reorder level is the quantity at which Orderly considers an item to be running low. If an item's stock falls to or below this number it will be flagged with a low stock warning. Leave the reorder level blank if you do not want low stock alerts for that item.

## The Low-Stock Alert View

Orderly uses color-coded badges to flag items that need attention. An **Out of Stock** badge (red) appears when an item's quantity is zero. A **Low Stock** badge (amber) appears when stock is at or below the reorder level. A **Critical Stock** badge (dark red) appears when stock has fallen to or below half of the reorder level.

Rows for out-of-stock items are visually dimmed in the table to help you distinguish them from items that are still available.

The Dashboard Home also surfaces your six lowest-stock items in the Inventory Alerts panel, sorted from least stock to most. This gives you a quick view of your most urgent inventory needs without having to open the full Inventory section.

## Linking Inventory Items to Products

Some inventory items are linked to specific products — for example, an ingredient like Milk may be connected to every product that uses it, such as Latte, Cappuccino, and Mocha. When an item is linked to products, those product names appear in the **Affects** column of the inventory list.

Opening the item's detail page shows a **Used In Products** card listing every product that depends on this item. This makes it easy to understand the downstream impact of a stock shortage — if Milk runs out, you can see immediately which products will be unavailable on the storefront.

When a linked item is out of stock or critically low, the Product Dependencies panel at the top of the inventory list will flag the affected products so you can act before customers are impacted.

# 8. Reports

## The Reports Dashboard

The Reports Dashboard is the central hub for all reporting and analytics features in Orderly. It opens with a bar chart showing your revenue by month for the current calendar year, giving you an at-a-glance view of how the business is performing across the year. Bars with no revenue are shown as empty — only months with at least one completed order are displayed with a value label.

Clicking any bar on the chart navigates directly to the Sales Summary page filtered to that month, so you can drill into a specific period without having to set filters manually.

Below the chart, four navigation cards link to the main reporting tools: **Sales Summary** for revenue and order totals, **Product Performance** for per-product trend data, **Export Data** for downloading reports as CSV files, and **Purchase Order** for generating a restock order based on low-stock inventory. Each of these is described in its own section of this manual.

## Sales Summary

The Sales Summary report shows your total revenue and order volume for a selected period, along with a ranked breakdown of every product's sales performance. At the top of the page, three summary tiles display **Total Revenue**, **Units Sold**, and your **Top Selling Product** for the period, including that product's total revenue and unit count.

Below the summary tiles, a bar chart visualises revenue over time. When no month filter is applied, the chart plots revenue by month across the selected year. When a specific month is selected, the chart switches to a daily view, showing revenue for each day within that month. Hovering over any bar shows a tooltip with the exact revenue and order count for that period.

The **Sales by Product** table below the chart lists every product and variant sold during the period, ranked by units sold. Each row shows the product name, variant, unit price, units sold, and total revenue generated. The top three rows are highlighted in gold, silver, and bronze to make your best performers easy to identify. You can sort the table by any column by clicking the column header, and filter the list by typing in the search bar.

Use the **Export** button to download the current report as a CSV file, or **Print** to send it to a printer or save it as a PDF.

## Product Performance

The Product Performance report lets you dig into the sales history of individual products. The default view is a ranked table of all products, showing each product's name, variant, category, units sold, and total revenue for the selected period. As with Sales Summary, the top three performers are highlighted in gold, silver, and bronze. You can sort by any column and filter by category using the category dropdown that appears when no individual product is selected.

To view the full performance history for a specific product, either click its row in the rankings table or select it from the product dropdown in the filter bar. This opens the product detail view, which shows three summary tiles — **Total Revenue**, **Units Sold**, and **Best Period** (the month or day with the highest revenue) — followed by a revenue trend chart and a full breakdown table. The breakdown table lists each period as a separate row with its unit and revenue totals, so you can see exactly how sales have been distributed over time.

The trend chart is monthly by default when viewing a full year, and switches to a daily breakdown when a specific month is selected. This allows you to identify which days within a month drove the most revenue for a given product. To return to the rankings table, clear the product selection from the dropdown or click the **× Clear Filters** button.

## Filtering by Date Range

Both Sales Summary and Product Performance share the same filtering system. A **Year** dropdown and a **Month** dropdown appear in the filter bar at the top of each page. The year dropdown is populated with all years that have at least one order, and the month dropdown shows only the months within the selected year that have order data.

Selecting a year without choosing a month shows year-to-date data for that year — the chart displays one bar per month, and the summary tiles and table reflect the full year. Selecting a month drills down to that specific month — the chart switches to a daily view, and all figures reflect that month's data only. Clearing the month filter reverts to the year view. Clearing both filters shows all-time data across every year on record.

The period label displayed below the filter bar always reflects the active selection, so you can confirm at a glance what data is being shown. To reset all filters at once, click the **× Clear Filters** button.

# 9. Exporting Data

## Exporting Orders to CSV

The Export Data page is accessible from the **Export Data** card on the Reports Dashboard, or from the **Export** button available on the Sales Summary and Product Performance pages. It provides three separate downloads — Orders, Products, and Inventory — each as a CSV file that can be opened in Excel, Google Sheets, or any other spreadsheet application.

The **Date Range** control at the top of the page applies exclusively to the Orders export. Use the start and end date fields to define the period you want to cover. The range defaults to the last 30 days each time you open the page. The selected date range is shown on the Orders export card so you can confirm it before downloading.

Once the date range is set, click **Download Orders CSV** to generate and download the file. The export includes all non-draft orders — Pending, Completed, and Cancelled — that fall within the selected period. Each row in the file contains the order date, customer name, order status, and the order total.

## Exporting Products to CSV

The Products export does not use a date filter — it always reflects the current state of your product catalog at the time of download. Click **Download Products CSV** to generate the file immediately.

Each row in the Products CSV represents one product variant and includes the product name, variant name, SKU, pricing, and current stock level. This export is useful for auditing your catalog, sharing a product list with a third party, or importing your data into another system.

## Exporting Inventory to CSV

Like the Products export, the Inventory export reflects the current live state of your stock and does not accept a date filter. Click **Download Inventory CSV** to download the file.

Each row in the Inventory CSV represents one inventory item and includes the item name, current stock quantity, unit of measure, and reorder level. This is useful for sharing a stock snapshot with a supplier, reviewing quantities in a spreadsheet, or keeping an off-system record of your inventory at a point in time.
